Accessible Software Engineering

1st Workshop on Accessible Software Engineering

Co-located with FSE 2026, Montreal, Canada

About the Workshop

Welcome to the first workshop on Accessible Software Engineering. This workshop provides an interdisciplinary venue for researchers and practitioners from software engineering, human-computer interaction (HCI), computer science education, and other relevant fields to share research, discuss ideas, and promote accessibility in software engineering. We plan to discuss and advance the integration of and development for accessibility into all aspects of the software engineering discipline.

This workshop will be utilized as a place for bringing together researchers to foster interdisciplinary, collaborative discussions. A core goal of this workshop is to establish a shared vocabulary for discussing disabilities within a software engineering context. We want all attendees to be informed of an overview of accessibility principles, resource guides on disability, and relevant global policies and legislation. To facilitate these goals, the workshop will consist of various forms of research presentations, keynote talks, a panel, and small group discussions designed to encourage deep engagement.

We hope this workshop will help attendees become familiar with ongoing research, identify pressing research topics, and shape novel methodologies, tools, and best practices that proactively ensure accessible software engineering environments and ensure that software is made accessible.

Target Audience

We welcome the attendance of anyone interested in making software engineering (the tools, processes, practices, and policies) accessible to people with disabilities. Researchers, practitioners, educators, and students are all encouraged to attend.

Call for Papers

We seek submissions covering a broad range of accessibility-related topics and encourage submissions from academics and industry practitioners on any aspects of accessibility within software engineering, including but not limited to:

Making Software Accessible to Users with Disabilities

  • Instructional methods, tools, and resources that teach software engineering students about accessibility
  • Open source tools for accessibility
  • Accessibility pragmatics for working with particular OSs, libraries, and tools.
  • Cross-platform engineering of accessible software
  • Supporting accessibility during software evolution
  • AI-based tools to detect and fix accessibility problems in software engineering
  • AI-based tools for translating accessibility regulations into practical coding tactics and code designs
  • Accessible software in specific domains (CAD, digital effects, etc)
  • Engineering design/architecture issues' impact on accessibility (e.g., accessible starvation opens a new window)
  • Experience Reports/Case studies in successful accessible software implementations

Making Software Development Inclusive to People with Disabilities

  • Mixed-ability software engineering collaborations
  • Software tool design according to social model of disability
  • Supporting intersectional disabilities in software teams (e.g., deaf-blind, chronic illness)
  • Supporting developers with cognitive differences
  • Career trajectory and outcomes for software engineers with disabilities
  • Policy work to support developers with disabilities (e.g., being on the WCAG board)
  • Recruitment and retention of software developers with disabilities

Types of Submissions

We welcome the following types of submissions:

  • Research papers addressing a topic related to accessibility and software engineering (up to 8 pages plus 2 pages references)
  • Experience reports providing anecdotal evidence about an experience related to accessibility in software engineering and interpreting the experience with actionable insights, advice, and lessons learned. This experience can be in an industry or academic setting (up to 8 pages plus 2 pages references)
  • Education papers relating to teaching accessibility in either an academic or an industry setting (up to 8 pages plus 2 pages references)
  • Position papers providing new ideas, emerging results, or interesting directions for an accessibility related topic (up to 4 pages plus 1 pages references)
  • Tool papers describing a tool designed to improve accessibility (up to 5 pages plus 1 page references)
  • Demonstrations of tools enable a practical 15-minute demonstration of an accessibility related tool including those still under development. Please provide a short abstract explaining the tool. (2 page abstract including references).
  • Tutorials to teach the community about an accessibility related topic in a session up to 30 minutes. Please provide a short abstract describing the topic, goal, and expected outcomes of the tutorial
  • Extended abstracts includes shorter research, experience, education, position, and tool papers (up to 5 pages including references). Extended abstracts are free of APC charges
